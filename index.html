Q1 — Warm-up
Tell me a bit about what you do professionally and where you're headed — career-wise, what's on your mind these days?
Listen for: current pain, ambition, openness to change
Q2 — Learning history
Have you ever taken a course, bootcamp, or training program to develop a professional skill? How did that go?
Listen for: bootcamp experience, frustration with online-only, what worked vs what did not
Q3 — Learning history
If you had to pick up a new technical skill today — say data analysis or digital marketing — how would you go about it?
Listen for: self-directed vs structured instinct, online vs in-person preference
Bootcamp Q1
Have you ever looked into a bootcamp — like DevPoint Labs, V School, Nucamp, or others? What made you consider it or not go for it?
Listen for: awareness of local options, pricing reaction, what attracted them, what stopped them. This tells you your main acquisition barrier or opportunity.
Bootcamp Q2
Do you know anyone who did a bootcamp? How did it go for them — did they actually land a job or use the skills?
Listen for: secondhand skepticism, success stories, word-of-mouth perception. Often more honest than asking about their own experience.
Bootcamp Q3
When you imagine an in-person class with 15–20 people, an instructor who works in the field, and a real project to build — how does that compare to what you have seen from bootcamps?
Listen for: immediate contrast with prior experiences, what excites vs. worries them. Surfaces the exact positioning language Harve should use.
Q4 — Logistics check
If there was an in-person program two evenings a week in Eagle Mountain or Lehi — say 6:30 to 9:30 PM — would that fit your life right now?
Utah-specific: commute tolerance, family dinner, church schedule (avoid Tue/Thu)
Q5 — Value & trust
What would this program need to offer or prove for you to actually sign up — not just consider it?
Listen for: job placement data, employer partnerships, alumni stories, refund guarantees
Q6 — Value & trust
What's the main thing that would stop you from doing it, even if it sounded great?
Listen for: time, cost, skepticism about outcomes, family pushback, financial risk
Q7 — Concept test
Four months, in-person twice a week evenings, real projects, instructors who work in the industry. Topics like data analytics, digital marketing, or AI automation. Pricing $3,500–$4,500. What is your gut reaction?
Let them react fully before probing. Then ask: "What would make that price feel worth it?"
